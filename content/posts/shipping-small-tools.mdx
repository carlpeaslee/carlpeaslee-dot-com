Big plans are seductive because they imply leverage. Small tools are better because they produce evidence.

When a workflow is still fuzzy, the best move is usually to build the narrowest thing that lets you exercise the loop end to end. That might be a tiny script, a lightweight UI, or a rough internal page.

The useful threshold is not polish. It is contact with reality.

## A practical rule

If a problem has not survived contact with a scrappy implementation, it has not earned a platform-level solution yet.

That rule cuts through a lot of unnecessary architecture. It also creates better writing material, because posts grounded in real use tend to say something concrete.
